# NHS A&E Performance — Presentation Script

## Opening — lead with the finding, not the dashboard

*(Pause. Make eye contact before the first line — don't open by looking at the screen.)*

"The NHS target is that 95% of A&E patients are seen within 4 hours. For the last three years, we've been at 74%. Not for one bad month — for three straight years, stuck in the same 70-to-78% band, with no real improvement at any point."

*(Pause. Let that sit for a second before continuing.)*

"That gap isn't just a statistic — it's longer waits, worse clinical outcomes, and the regulatory and reputational risk that falls on the trusts furthest behind. I'm going to walk you through three findings from this data, and what I think we should do about each one."

## Finding 1 — We are not improving

Looking at the trend over the last three years, performance oscillates between roughly 70% and 78% — it never gets close to the 95% target, and there's no consistent upward trajectory. Whatever interventions have been tried nationally over this period have not shifted the underlying number.

*(Evidence: the trend line chart, with the 95% target marked for reference.)*

**So what:** this isn't a temporary dip to monitor — it's a sustained, structural performance gap that needs a different kind of intervention than "wait and see."

## Finding 2 — A small number of hospitals need urgent attention, and one is worth studying

When the comparison is restricted to genuine acute hospitals — excluding small minor-injury units and walk-in clinics, which trivially hit 100% because they only see a handful of low-acuity patients a day — a real, meaningful spread emerges. Most major trusts sit in the amber zone, several percentage points below target but not in crisis. None are currently flagged red in this view, which is itself useful to know — but the more interesting finding is the opposite end: **Mid Yorkshire Hospitals NHS Trust is the one trust in this comparison meeting the 95% target.**

**So what:** rather than only asking "who needs the most help," it's worth asking what Mid Yorkshire is doing differently — staffing model, patient flow, discharge process — that the other major trusts aren't. That's a more actionable question than just funding the worst performers.

*(Evidence: the trust comparison chart, RAG-coded, filtered to exclude small units for a fair comparison.)*

## Finding 3 — Demand is concentrated in working-age adults, not just the elderly

The data shows attendances are heavily concentrated in the 25-44 and 45-64 age bands — not the elderly population that often dominates the public narrative around A&E pressure. This has direct implications for staffing mix, triage design, and where public health messaging should be targeted.

*(Evidence: the age demographic breakdown chart.)*

## Recommendation

"Based on this, I'd recommend three things, in priority order:"

1. **"Study Mid Yorkshire first."** It's the one trust meeting target in this view. Before spending anywhere else, I'd want to know what's structurally different about their staffing model or patient flow — that's the cheapest possible win, because it's already proven to work somewhere in the system.
2. **"Flag the amber trusts for active monitoring, not passive reporting."** Three flat years tells us these trusts will not self-correct on their own. Waiting for next quarter's numbers to "see if it improves" has already been tried — for three years.
3. **"Adopt this as the standing monthly review tool."** It's not a one-off slide deck — it sits on a pipeline that refreshes automatically. The conversation we're having today should be one we have every month, with current numbers, not a retrospective.

## The ask

*(Always end by asking for something concrete — don't just trail off after the recommendations.)*

"What I'd like from this group is a decision on two things: first, sign-off to formally request Mid Yorkshire's operational data for a comparison study; and second, agreement that this dashboard becomes the standing monthly performance review, rather than something we revisit only when there's a problem."

## If asked: limitations

"One thing I'll flag proactively: clicking a specific trust doesn't yet re-filter the demographic breakdown chart — those two data sources come from different original NHS reports and don't share a common organisation code reference yet. That's scoped, known follow-up work, not a hidden gap — happy to take questions on the technical side if useful."
